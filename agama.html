Religious Visits

Exploring the spiritual heritage of Yogyakarta

Gereja Santa Maria Assumpta Pakem

On the second day of our Live-In experience, we had the opportunity to visit Santa Maria Assumpta Church in Pakem, located close to Pentingsari Village. The church’s architecture is a beautiful blend of modern design and traditional Javanese elements, offering a sacred and peaceful atmosphere that remains deeply connected to the local culture.

What makes this church special is the presence of an old well that has existed for many decades. This well was used to help the locals get water during the 2010 Mount Merapi eruption.

Gereja Hati Kudus Tuhan Yesus Ganjuran

A few days later, we made a visit to Gereja Hati Kudus Tuhan Yesus in Ganjuran, a site known for its distinct and deeply rooted Javanese architectural style. Surrounded by lush greenery, the church offers a refreshing and serene atmosphere. It was originally built in 1924 by the Schmutzer family, Dutch sugar factory owners who deeply admired and respected Javanese culture.

Gereja Hati Kudus Tuhan Yesus in Ganjuran is a remarkable Catholic pilgrimage site that features a unique Javanese-style temple (candi) dedicated to the Sacred Heart of Jesus. This sacred complex beautifully combines Javanese Hindu-Buddhist architectural elements with Catholic devotion.

What impressed me most was the candi-shaped chapel where the altar is designed like a traditional Javanese pendopo (open pavilion). The statues of Jesus and Mary are dressed in Javanese royal attire, symbolizing how the Catholic faith has been inculturated into Javanese traditions.
