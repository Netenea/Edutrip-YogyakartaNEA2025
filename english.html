A cultural immersion experience in the heart of Yogyakarta

The Hidden Beauty of Desa Pentingsari

Have you ever been to a place where time seems to slow down, where every breath feels fresher, and every smile feels warmer? That's what we felt during our Yogyakarta Edutrip to Desa Pentingsari, a hidden gem in Sleman, Yogyakarta. Our school experienced a Live In there, and it was unforgettable.

Desa Pentingsari's environment

We did activities like farming, cooking, learning traditional dances, and playing traditional music. The villagers proudly preserve their culture and warmly share it with visitors. Desa Pentingsari is very big and full of amazing natural beauty, surrounded by rice fields, trees, rivers, and fresh air, the village felt like a dream. When we arrived, We were greeted warmly with big smiles that made us feel welcome right away. It was a great first impression and made us excited to learn more about their village. Desa Pentingsari is full of culture, beauty, and kindness, making it a perfect place to refresh and have fun.

Where Community Feels Like Family

"The people of Desa Pentingsari were more than just friendly—they were like family."

The people of Desa Pentingsari were more than just friendly—they were like family. From the first moment, their warmth wrapped around us like a cozy blanket. They showed us so much kindness, care, and patience. What amazed us the most was their kindness during the fasting month. Even though they were fasting and could not eat or drink during the day, they still prepared and offered us lots of delicious food and snacks without hesitation.

I stayed with Mr. Sugeng and Mrs. Sarthi, two of the kindest people I've ever met. Every day, they woke up at around 5 a.m. to start their daily activities, such as cleaning the house, preparing food, running their "warteg", and many more. Their lifestyle was simple but full of heart. Despite working hard until 10 p.m., they always made time for us. I was touched by how selfless and dedicated they were.

Living like a Local in Desa Pentingsari

Living in Desa Pentingsari was very fun, peaceful, and we felt so free. At sunrise, the village awoke to the songs of birds and the whisper of the wind through the trees. Every day felt like a new adventure as we explored the local culture and daily life.

We tried many new things, like planting rice, plowing the rice fields with cows, catching fish with our hands, and cooking traditional foods and snacks. We also helped our "foster" parents with their daily jobs, like sell food in their warteg (small food stall), and cleaned the house or did simple chores. These small things helped us feel like a real part of their family.

When we had free time, me and my friends explored the village and played like local kids. We ran around in the mud, splashed in the river, played in the rain, and even had fun walks in the middle of the night. Every moment was full of laughter, friendship, and adventure.

A Village Full of Culture and Beauty

In Desa Pentingsari, there are so many cultures that they protect and live with. Desa Pentingsari is a treasure chest of culture. During our Live In experience, we visited local UMKM (small businesses), where people make handmade products and delicious food that show their culture. We also learned to play the gamelan and traditional dances, which was fun and exciting.

Plowing the rice fields was one of the most unforgettable traditions we tried, which is important for their food and is a tradition they have kept for many years. As we spent more time in the village, we saw how the locals live each day, from warm greetings to caring for animals, which showed how much they value their traditions. The people of Desa Pentingsari live in harmony with each other and with nature, making the village rich in culture and meaning.
