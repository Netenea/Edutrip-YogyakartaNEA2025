About My Journey

An Unforgettable Experience with SMP Santa Laurensia

A really memorable trip with SMP Santa Laurensia that allowed me to immerse myself in Javanese culture, history, and traditions with. From the majestic temples to the vibrant street life, every moment was a lesson in the rich heritage of this special region.

Through this portfolio, I invite you to join me as I recount my adventures, share my photographs, and reflect on what made this journey so meaningful to me.

Tourist Destinations

Explore the amazing places I visited during my time in Yogyakarta

Prambanan Temple

4.7 (98,082 reviews)

We visited the tallest temple in Indonesia, The Prambanan Temple, a magnificent Hindu temple complex with towering spires and intricate bas-reliefs.

Highlights

  • Sendratari Ramayana performance
  • Main Brahma, Shiva, Vishnu temple
  • Tour inside the temple
View Photos

Sendratari Ramayana

4.5 (790 reviews)

The Sendratari Ramayana is a breathtaking dance-drama that brings the Ramayana epic to life through Javanese dance, gamelan music, and stunning choreography, set against the majestic backdrop of Indonesia’s Prambanan Temple.

Highlights

  • Beautiful Story of Ramayana
  • Mezmerizing choreography
  • Breath-taking of Prambanan Temple as the background
View Photos

Keraton Yogyakarta

4.6 (37,652 reviews)

Keraton Yogyakarta is the royal palace of the Sultan of Yogyakarta, blending Javanese culture, history, and architecture. It remains a political and cultural center, showcasing traditional art, rituals, and heritage.

Highlights

  • Rich culture
  • Many traditional and ancient objects
  • Daily cultural performances
View Photos

Gereja Hati Kudus Yesus Ganjuran

4.9 (15,000 reviews)

Gereja Hati Kudus Yesus Ganjuran is a unique Catholic church in Yogyakarta, blending Javanese architecture with Christian traditions. Built in 1924, it features a temple-shaped shrine and is a popular pilgrimage site.

Highlights

  • Blends Javanese culture with Catholic culture
  • Peaceful and quiet environment
  • Sacred Heart of Jesus temple
View Photos

Museum Ullen Sentalu

4.7 (10,649 reviews)

Museum Ullen Sentalu in Yogyakarta showcases the rich Javanese culture, art, and royal heritage. Known for its unique architecture and guided tours, it offers a deep insight into the life of Javanese royalty, traditional batik, and historical artifacts.

Highlights

  • Javanese Royal Heritage
  • Unique Architecture and Atmosphere
  • Deep Javanese culture
View Photos

Museum Gunung Merapi

4.4 (8,400 reviews)

Museum Gunung Api Merapi in Yogyakarta showcases the history and geology of Mount Merapi, featuring interactive exhibits and volcanic artifacts. During our visit, we also enjoyed a thrilling Jeep tour up the volcano, exploring lava fields and eruption sites.

Highlights

  • Lava Jeep Tour to Mount Merapi
  • Explore the amazing museum
  • Many interesting facts about Mount Merapi
View Photos

Museum Manusia Purba Sangiran Krikilan

4.6 (1,700 reviews)

Museum Manusia Purba Sangiran Klaster Krikilan in Central Java showcases Homo erectus fossils and prehistoric artifacts, offering insights into early human evolution. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it’s a must-visit for history lovers.

Highlights

  • Home to the largest Homo Erectus Fossils collection
  • Many Prehistoric Artifacts
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
View Photos

Museum De Tjolomadoe Solo

4.6 (21,00 reviews)

Museum De Tjolomadoe in Solo is a former sugar factory turned museum, showcasing the history of Indonesia’s sugar industry. With its industrial-style architecture, interactive exhibits, and preserved machinery, it offers a unique blend of history, culture, and modern attractions.

Highlights

  • Historic Sugar Factory
  • Preserved Machinery
  • Modern Attractions
View Photos

Malioboro

3.8 (2,849 reviews)

Malioboro is the iconic shopping street of Yogyakarta, known for its lively atmosphere, street vendors, and cultural charm. Lined with traditional markets, local crafts, and street food, it’s the perfect place to experience the city's vibrant heritage and nightlife.

Highlights

  • Shopping Paradise
  • Street Food Delights
  • Lively Atmosphere
View Photos

Places We Stayed

Discover the wonderful accommodations we experienced in Yogyakarta

Live In Desa Wisata Pentingsari Yogyakarta

Sleman, Yogyakarta

Desa Wisata Pentingsari in Yogyakarta is a charming eco-tourism village where visitors can experience Javanese rural life firsthand. Through the Live-In program, guests stay in villagers' homes, participating in traditional farming, cultural activities, and nature exploration, creating an immersive and authentic experience.

Live In Beautiful natural environment Cultural activities

Grand Mercure Yogyakarta Adi Sucipto

Caturtunggal, Yogyakarta

Grand Mercure Yogyakarta Adi Sucipto is a luxurious hotel offering modern comforts with Javanese elegance. Strategically located near the airport and city center, it features spacious rooms, a rooftop pool, fine dining, and excellent hospitality, making it ideal for both leisure and business travelers.

Borobudur Designs Combined Java and French culture interior Swimming Pool Spa

Novotel Solo

Banjarsari, Surakarta City

Novotel Solo is a modern yet culturally rich hotel in the heart of Solo, blending contemporary comfort with Javanese charm. Featuring spacious rooms, a relaxing pool, spa, and authentic local dining, it’s perfect for both business and leisure travelers seeking a convenient and elegant stay.

Strategic Location Outdoor Swimming Pools Family-Friendly Atmosphere
